Baursak

Baursak (also spelled boortsog or baursaki) is a traditional fried dough food commonly found in Central Asian cuisines, particularly in Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, and Mongolia. It is made from a simple dough consisting of flour, yeast, sugar, salt, and water or milk. The dough is rolled out, cut into small pieces or shapes (often squares or diamonds), and then deep-fried until golden brown and puffy.
